01About

Executive Profile

For nearly nineteen years, Beshir Saad Salem has been the steady hand behind the instrumentation that keeps Ras Lanuf's process units measuring, signaling, and running true.

Beginning as an Instrumentation & Control Technician in 2007, he built a career on the unglamorous discipline that production depends on: a transmitter calibrated correctly, a gas chromatograph reading clean, a shutdown completed on schedule. By 2020 that discipline had carried him to Process Analytical Instruments Specialist and Supervisor — the engineer trusted with the plant's most sensitive measurement loops, from Siemens MAXUM gas chromatographs to Yokogawa pH and conductivity analyzers.

Today, as Acting Head of the Instrumentation Division, his responsibility has widened from the instrument loop to the division itself: leading technical evaluations, planning modernization projects, negotiating with vendors, and mentoring the technicians who will carry this discipline forward. He has overseen system upgrades from Bently Nevada vibration monitoring to radar tank gauging, each one a step in RASCO's broader digital transformation.

He treats reliability as a craft and continuous learning as a habit — closing the loop, literally and otherwise, between engineering studies, field execution, and the next problem worth solving. He is now exploring how artificial intelligence and digital tools can sharpen that craft further, without ever losing sight of the instrument at the end of the wire.
